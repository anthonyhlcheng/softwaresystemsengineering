Digital Music Lab

Currently, there are a few projects being carried out by UCL but here we have decided to focus on one - Digital Music Labs.

Digital music labs is a Digital Transformations project that is carried out in UCL. It aims to develop software that explores and analysis music collections. It also aims to provide researchers with tools to analyse music audio, scores and metadata.

One of these tools is the DML web interface. It can explore, analyse and compare music collections and recordings from the British Library’s Sound Archives, CHARM and I Like Music. It can be used to compare data between different composers, for example you can compare which keys composers tend to use. You can compare qualities such as tempo, key and relative chord sequences. It also allows you to compare individual pieces.

Another is a chord sequence visualisation tool. It produces a geometric representation of chord progressions. DML has extracted chord sequence patterns from over a million tracks to use as data. It creates a visual aid to explore the data.

One project that the Digital music lab is working on is the “An Integrated Audio-Symbolic Model of Music Similarity”. This project aims to use the tools created form the DML project to answer the question of what contributes to the similarity of music.
